Research Programme

A topos-theoretic approach to AI agent safety: from mathematical foundations to benchmark-validated systems.

About

The central question is: can we give AI agents formal safety guarantees that compose over arbitrary traces? The approach rests on two mathematical foundations: a topos-theoretic account of how observation and concepts relate, and a coalgebraic treatment of processes that never stabilise. From these, it passes through three concrete sub-problems identified in the AGI foundations literature and arrives at a single implementation validated on independent benchmarks. See also: origin.

Research direction

  • Open-source release of TTGOS Guard and Evidence Pack schema
  • Formalise the admissible-update axioms and witness schema in a proof assistant; prove the core lemma (step-safety implies trace-safety)
  • Extend compositional safety guarantees to long-horizon, multi-step workflows with counterexample-driven refinement
  • Abstract the framework to span both discrete agent traces and continuous dynamical models, connecting with formal verification methods for continuous systems
