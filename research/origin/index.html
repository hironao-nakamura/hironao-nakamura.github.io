Origin

The work did not begin from AI safety. It began on a quiet night in the summer of 2019, on the terrace of a homestay in Colorado. I was there with a friend, in our last summer before graduation. An intuition took shape: the first-person world, the raw phenomenal field you are witnessing right now, is structurally primary. The familiar "objective" world, where objects exist in a pre-given space, is a model built on top of it. Category theory, it seemed, could express this structure directly.

Over years of living with this idea, the intuition crystallised into a precise architecture. Observation and concepts each form their own mathematical universe (topos), and the morphism connecting them, the grounding morphism, is what makes compositional guarantees possible at all.

This framework did not remain theoretical. When implemented as a runtime safety layer for AI agents, the architecture outperformed existing approaches across multiple independent benchmarks, not because safety was the goal, but because it was a structural consequence of the design. That empirical confirmation turned a mathematical insight into a full research programme.
